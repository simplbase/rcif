# Glossary

## Asset Layer

The infrastructure layer responsible for regulated token lifecycle operations including issuance, transfer, redemption, freeze, recovery, and burn processes.

---

## Claim

A verifiable assertion associated with an identity, typically issued by a trusted claim issuer.

Examples include KYC verification, AML verification, accreditation status, or jurisdiction eligibility.

---

## Claim Issuer

An entity authorized to issue verifiable claims associated with participant identities.

---

## Claim Topic

A classification identifier representing the type of claim being issued or verified.

---

## Compliance Layer

The infrastructure layer responsible for enforcing regulatory restrictions, transfer validation rules, and participant eligibility requirements.

---

## Identity Layer

The infrastructure layer responsible for participant identity management, ONCHAINID orchestration, claim ownership, and identity lifecycle operations.

---

## Modular Compliance

A composable compliance architecture model where individual compliance rules are implemented as independent enforcement modules.

---

## ONCHAINID

A decentralized identity framework commonly used within ERC-3643 ecosystems for identity ownership and claim verification.

---

## RCIF

Regulatory Compliance Infrastructure Framework.

An implementation and operational framework for compliant digital asset infrastructure systems.

---

## Trusted Issuer

A claim issuer recognized and authorized by the infrastructure governance model for compliance verification purposes.

---

## Transfer Validation

The process of verifying whether a token transfer satisfies all required compliance and eligibility conditions before execution.
