# Identity Layer

## Overview

The Identity Layer is responsible for managing participant identity infrastructure within regulated digital asset ecosystems.

RCIF treats identity as a foundational infrastructure component required for compliance enforcement, participant eligibility verification, claim management, and operational governance.

The layer primarily focuses on ONCHAINID-based identity architectures and related claim verification systems.

---

## Objectives

The Identity Layer aims to standardize:

- Identity ownership models
- ONCHAINID deployment patterns
- Claim issuer relationships
- Claim lifecycle management
- Identity verification workflows
- Recovery and replacement procedures
- Trusted issuer coordination
- Operational governance practices

---

## Core Components

The Identity Layer typically includes:

- ONCHAINID contracts
- Identity registries
- Trusted issuer registries
- Claim topic registries
- Claim issuers
- Identity management services

---

## Identity Ownership

Each participant identity should maintain a clear ownership and control model.

Identity ownership models may include:

- Self-managed identities
- Custodial identities
- Institution-managed identities
- Recovery-enabled identity structures

Operational responsibilities and recovery procedures should be clearly defined during onboarding.

---

## Claim Issuers

Claim issuers are responsible for issuing verifiable claims associated with participant identities.

Examples include:

- KYC verification providers
- AML verification providers
- Accredited investor verification entities
- Jurisdiction verification providers
- Institutional compliance authorities

Claim issuer trust relationships should be explicitly governed and operationally auditable.

---

## Claim Lifecycle

Claims generally follow the following lifecycle:

1. Identity creation
2. Participant verification
3. Claim issuance
4. Claim registration
5. Compliance verification
6. Claim expiration or revocation
7. Claim renewal or replacement

Operational systems should maintain visibility into claim validity and issuer status.

---

## Identity Recovery

Identity recovery procedures should be standardized and operationally documented.

Recovery workflows may involve:

- Wallet replacement
- Identity reassignment
- Claim migration
- Administrative approval procedures
- Multi-party authorization flows

Recovery operations should maintain auditability and minimize unauthorized identity transfer risk.

---

## Operational Considerations

Operational concerns include:

- Identity deployment sequencing
- Claim synchronization
- Issuer trust management
- Registry consistency
- Recovery coordination
- Administrative oversight
- Audit logging

Identity infrastructure should be treated as long-term operational infrastructure rather than temporary onboarding configuration.

---

## Design Goal

The goal of the Identity Layer is to provide a standardized operational identity framework that supports compliant, auditable, and maintainable regulated digital asset ecosystems.
